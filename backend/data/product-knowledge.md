## What is SAS Edu AI?

SAS Edu AI is a live classroom engagement platform built specifically for SASTRA University. It helps teachers reach every student in the room — not just the ones who raise their hands. Students stay engaged, teachers get real insight into what's actually being understood, and learning becomes a two-way conversation instead of a one-way lecture.

## Core Features

### 1. Live Polls
Teachers can launch quick polls at any point during a lecture. Every student responds instantly from their phone or laptop — no app download needed, just a browser. Teachers see results in real time, spot misconceptions the moment they form, and can adjust their teaching on the spot. No more asking "any doubts?" and getting silence.

### 2. AI Study Assistant
Students can ask questions about course material at any time — even at 2AM before an exam. The AI assistant answers only from the teacher's own uploaded materials, not from the internet. This keeps answers accurate, syllabus-specific, and relevant to exactly what was taught. Teachers upload their slides or notes once, and students get a 24/7 personal tutor for that content.

### 3. Auto Quiz Generation
Upload a lecture file and the platform automatically generates multiple-choice questions from it. Teachers can use these for practice quizzes, revision sessions, or in-class assessments — without spending hours writing questions manually.

### 4. Gamification & Motivation
Students earn XP points and badges for participating — answering polls correctly, scoring well on quizzes, maintaining participation streaks. A live leaderboard shows class rankings, making learning competitive and fun. Badges include things like Quiz Master, Streak King, and Top Scholar.

### 5. Analytics & Insights for Teachers
After every session, teachers get a detailed breakdown: which topics confused students the most, which questions had the lowest accuracy, who dropped off during the lecture, and how engagement compares to previous sessions. This helps teachers know exactly where to spend more time — without guessing.

### 6. Rich Question Types
Beyond standard multiple-choice, the platform supports assertion-reason questions, match-the-following, ordering, multi-correct answers, and negative marking — the kinds of question formats used in competitive exams and university assessments.

### 7. Simple Three-Step Setup
1. Upload your lecture file (PDF, Word doc, or PowerPoint)
2. Create a session — the platform generates a 6-digit code
3. Share the code with students — they join instantly from any browser

No app to install, no IT department involved, no complicated onboarding. It takes about two minutes to get started.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do students need to download an app?**
No. Everything works in any web browser on any device — phone, tablet, or laptop.

**Does the AI assistant use the internet to answer questions?**
No. It answers only from the materials the teacher has uploaded. This keeps answers relevant to the syllabus and prevents students from getting off-topic information.

**What file types can teachers upload?**
PDF, Word documents (.docx), and PowerPoint presentations (.pptx).

**Is student data private?**
Yes. Access is restricted to authenticated SASTRA members only. Teachers see their own students' data; students only see their own performance.

**Can it be used for any subject?**
Yes — engineering, sciences, humanities, management, any subject. The AI adapts to whatever material is uploaded.

**What if a student misses a class?**
They can use the AI study assistant later to catch up on the uploaded material and test themselves with the auto-generated quizzes.

**Can teachers see which students are confused?**
Yes. The analytics dashboard shows topic-by-topic confusion rates and flags which students had the lowest accuracy on specific questions.

**Is there a limit on how many students can join a session?**
The platform is designed to handle full classroom sizes comfortably.

**Who built this?**
SAS Edu AI was built at SASTRA University, in Thanjavur — by people who understand the classroom challenges here firsthand. It's not a generic product adapted for Indian classrooms; it was built for them from the ground up.
